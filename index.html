LUISA ARISTIZÁBAL

CAPAS DE

MEMORIA

INSTALACIÓN

 

Memory is an intangible that we often see, remember and hear; however, the recurring thing is oblivion. Among the variables of memory is that which time has turned into heritage even though it tends to fall into oblivion. Finding memory, distinguishing it, working with it, creating works of art, precise study, constancy, research and method. Episodic memory, the method employed by Luisa Aristizábal, a master of the plastic arts, allows us to establish a layered relationship with people, facts, places, houses, streets, where traces of time and space are still preserved.

That same memory led the artist from the field to the experimentation with lands and pigments that, applied with paraffin, graphite or resin on paper, using techniques of engraving, watercolor or montages with found objects, confer to the work the value of patrimonial memory recovered. Observation and experimentation in the plastic process of the artist, have as a result the evolution of the memory / image relationship, which goes from deterioration, to disappearance to return to its recovery as patrimonial testimony.

The work of Luisa Aristizábal is the representation of oblivion that memory recovers. Memory that, sometimes, according to an anonymous poet: it makes one cry, but also makes one laugh.

Saúl Alvarez Lara
